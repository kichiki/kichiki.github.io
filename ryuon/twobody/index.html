Appendix

Here, we study two sheres of radii $a_1$ and $a_2$ at $\mathbf{x}_1$ and $\mathbf{x}_2$. Following Jeffrey and Onishi (1984), we use two non-dimensional parameters
$\displaystyle{ s = \frac{2r}{a_1 + a_2},\quad \lambda = \frac{a_2}{a_1}, }$
where $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{x}_2 - \mathbf{x}_1$. Resistance problem is given by the resistance matrix
$\displaystyle{ \left[\begin{array}{c} \mathbf{F}\\ \mathbf{T}\\ \mathbf{S} \end{array}\right] = \mu \left[\begin{array}{ccc} \mathbf{A} & \tilde{\mathbf{B}} & \tilde{\mathbf{G}}\\ \mathbf{B} & \mathbf{C} & \tilde{\mathbf{H}}\\ \mathbf{G} & \mathbf{H} & \mathbf{M} \end{array}\right] \cdot \left[\begin{array}{c} \mathbf{U}\\ \mathbf{\Omega}\\ \mathbf{E} \end{array}\right] , }$
where each vector on both sides has elements for two particles and each sub-matrix has four sub-components of all particle-particle pairs, 11, 12, 21, and 22. From the symmetry, those resistance matrices are given by
$ \begin{eqnarray} A_{ij}^{\alpha\beta} &=& X_{\alpha\beta}^A e_i e_j + Y_{\alpha\beta}^A (\delta_{ij} + e_i e_j),\\ B_{ij}^{\alpha\beta} &=& Y_{\alpha\beta}^B \varepsilon_{ijk} e_k,\\ C_{ij}^{\alpha\beta} &=& X_{\alpha\beta}^C e_i e_j + Y_{\alpha\beta}^C (\delta_{ij} + e_i e_j), \end{eqnarray} }$
where $\mathbf{e} = \mathbf{r} / r$.
$ \begin{eqnarray} X_{11}^{A}(s,\lambda) & = & \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} f_{2k}(\lambda) (1+\lambda)^{-2k} s^{-2k},\\ X_{12}^{A}(s,\lambda) &=& \frac{-2}{1+\lambda} \sum_{k=0}^\infty f_{2k+1}(\lambda) (1+\lambda)^{-2k-1} s^{-2k-1}, \end{eqnarray} }$
where
$\displaystyle{ f_k(\lambda) = 2^k \sum_{q=0}^k P_{1,k-q,q} \lambda^q. }$
